Course Description

The objective of this class is to give students a hands-on introduction to the fundamentals of image processing. A variety of image processing techniques and applications will be discussed including image enhancement, noise removal, spatial domain and frequency domain filtering, image restoration, color image processing, image compression, edge detection and image segmentation.

Prerequisites

Prerequisite: CSCE 4813

This course requires familarity with computer graphics, linear algebra, calculus, and programming.

Programming Language: students can use any programming language, e.g. python, C/C++, Matlab, Java, for their assignments and projects.

Textbook

Readings will be assigned from the following textbooks:

  • Digital Image Processing (4th Edition), by Rafael C. Gonzalez and Richard E. Woods.
  • Digital Image Processing (3rd Edition), by Rafael C. Gonzalez and Richard E. Woods.
  • Computer Vision: Algorithms and Applications, by Richard Szeliski (free online).

Relevant research papers will be introduced in each lecture. Students are highly recommended to read the textbooks and the assigned research papers.

The following textbooks can also be useful references but are not required:

  • Principles of Digital Image Processing, by Burger and Burge.
  • Two-dimensional signal and image processing, by Jae S. Lim.
  • Computer Vision: A Modern Approach, by David Forsyth and Jean Ponce.
