Course Description

This course aims to provides an introduction to formal techniques for analyzing the complexity of algorithms. The course surveys important classes of algorithms used in computer science and engineering. Topics include: Algorithm Complexity Analysis, Searching, Sorting, Trees, Hashing, Huffman Coding Trees, Graphs and their applications (to be updated).

    • Textbook

    Most important materials will be covered in slides/lectures. There is no perfect textbook for this class, but there are some relevant books:

    • Introduction to Algorithms, the 3rd Edition (The MIT Press), by Thomas H. Cormen.
    • Data Structures and Algorithm Analysis in C++, 4th Ed. by Weiss.
    • Data Structures and Algorithms in C++, 2nd Edition by Michael T. Goodrich.
    • Cracking the Coding Interview by G. McDowell .

    Relevant research papers will be introduced in each lecture. Students are recommended to read the textbooks and the assigned research papers.
